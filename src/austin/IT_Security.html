IT Security, also known as Cybersecurity or Computer Security, is the branch of IT that focuses on and specializes in the protection of Computer Systems and Networks from aspects that include information disclosure, theft or damage to their hardware, software, and electronic data. Cybersecurity professionals use a variety of programming languages for their tasks. The top programming languages that are used in Cybersecurity include Python, Shell Scripting, HTML, JavaScript, and SQL.

With the tasks and responsibilities that IT Security professionals have, they must have the knowledge of the field and its key aspects. Due to the high importance of IT Security professionals and protecting computers and networks, IT Security professionals must have a solid grasp and understanding of how to do what is required of them. It is said that having a Bachelor's degree in a related field of IT Security is often required by most Cybersecurity positions from the entry-level and up to go along with one or more professional certificates. But for those who seek the highest levels of Cybersecurity and wish to have a long career in the profession, a Master's Degree is also highly recommended.

Technology is continuing to make a huge impact on our everyday lives, which means that the demand for more IT Security positions will increase. The employment of Information Security is projected to grow by 33% from 2020 to 2030, which is much faster than the typical average for all occupations. Around 16,300 job openings for Information Security are projected for each year, on average, over the decade.
The Salary of those who work in Cybersecurity is very high. In the United States, the top five states that pay those who work in Cybersecurity the most include New York, New Hampshire, California, Vermont, Idaho. New York is paying those who work in Cybersecurity $60.42 hourly, $2,417 weekly, $10,427 monthly, and $125,664 annually. New Hampshire is paying those who work in Cybersecurity $58.54 hourly, $2,342 weekly, $10,147 monthly, and $121,765 annually. California is paying those who work in Cybersecurity $57.94 hourly, $2,318 weekly, $10,043 monthly, and $120,520 annually. Vermont is paying those who work in Cybersecurity $55.31 hourly, $2,212 weekly, $9,587 monthly, and $115,042 annually. And in Idaho, they are paying those who work in Cybersecurity $54.59 hourly, $2,183 weekly, $9,462 monthly, and $113,540 annually.
