Bank Teller

What kind of companies are hiring and what kind of education do you need to work there?

Companies such as Mobile Teller, TD Bank, and Robert Half are willing to hire people applying for the position of bank teller. However, it is required that you have a high school diploma and a bachelor's degree.

What are their locations, schedules, and payment?

Considering the job we are talking about takes place in banks, it’s obvious that these are basically everywhere in every country that has banks. The schedule is usually about 8 hours per day if we disregard night shifts.

What are the qualifications and duties in the workplace?

The qualifications needed are cash handling experience and on-the-job training, ability to pass a background check, exceptional time management, exceptional communication, exceptional customer service skills, basic math and computer skills, high level of accountability, high level of efficiency, high level of accuracy, strong sales skills, and professional appearance and courteous manner. Duties in the workplace include providing professional teller services to the bank’s customers by prompt acknowledgment of the customer, maintaining a friendly and courteous demeanor, ensuring customer satisfaction on transaction requests and presenting a professional image, accurately and efficiently completing customer transactions (cashing checks, receiving deposits, savings account transactions, change orders, processing transfers, loan payments, redeeming bonds, cash advances, selling travelers checks, cashier’s checks and money orders, stop payments and wire transfer requests), following department policies and procedures, following regulations and security procedures, completing necessary documents and maintaining customer confidentiality, accurately and efficiently completing Currency Transaction Reports and Reg CC forms, responsible for achieving performance standards for referrals and cross-sell of products and services to achieve bank growth objectives, processing mail, processing night depository, processing ATM deposits, balancing the cash drawer and maintains control and accounting of the bank’s cash assets, assisting the supervisor in completing special day-end processing and daily reports, and depositing services and other clerical duties based on branch staffing requirements.
